# Insights Memo — theLook eCommerce Marketing Analysis

**To:** Marketing Leadership
**From:** Marketing Analytics
**Re:** Where to focus marketing investment — findings from an analysis of $8M in realized sales

---

## Summary

An analysis of ~125,000 orders and ~80,000 customers points to one clear conclusion: **theLook's growth problem is not acquisition — it's retention and conversion.** We acquire customers efficiently and at consistent value across channels, but we lose them at checkout and rarely bring them back. Investment should shift accordingly.

## What the data shows

**We lose more revenue at checkout than anywhere else.**
58% of sessions that add an item to cart never purchase — a far bigger leak than any other funnel step. This is the single highest-leverage place to recover revenue.

**Our acquisition channels are interchangeable on value.**
Search, Organic, Facebook, Email, and Display all produce roughly the same per-customer value (~$122). The difference between the "best" and "worst" channel is statistically insignificant (overlapping 95% confidence intervals). Search looks dominant only because it brings the most customers — not better ones.

**A small group of customers carries the business.**
Champions are 26% of customers but 44% of revenue. Another third of revenue belongs to high-value customers who have gone quiet (no purchase in 1.5–3.7 years).

**Customers rarely return.**
Only 1–2% of each monthly cohort purchases again the following month, and this hasn't improved over time. We are effectively a one-purchase business.

**We can't predict value upfront.**
A predictive model using channel and demographics could not distinguish future high-value customers from others (AUC 0.52, near chance). Value is created through the customer relationship, not determined at signup.

## Recommended actions

1. **Reduce cart abandonment** (highest priority). Abandoned-cart email/retargeting flows, a simplified checkout, and clearer shipping/payment information.
2. **Launch win-back campaigns** for At-Risk and Cant-Lose-Them segments — ~34% of revenue is tied up in lapsed high spenders.
3. **Rebalance acquisition spend toward lowest-cost channels** (Email, Organic). Since value is flat, the cheapest channel wins.
4. **Build a second-purchase program** — onboarding sequences and first-repeat incentives to lift our near-zero repeat rate.

## Caveat

This analysis uses a synthetic dataset; some absolute figures (overall conversion rate, retention levels) reflect how the data is generated rather than real customer behavior. The *relative* findings and analytical approach hold, and would be validated against live data and channel cost figures (to compute true CAC/ROAS) in a production setting.
